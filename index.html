01
Customer service

Reducing missed enquiries at a retail business

Bang Badar  ·  Gorontalo, Indonesia

Context

Daily enquiries arrived via walk-in and WhatsApp with no tracking system. Shift changes happened without handover notes, causing customers to repeat their issues multiple times.

Problem

A regular customer's exchange request went unanswered for four days. Her message had been read but never logged — staff were unaware and no follow-up was made.

Intervention

Set up a WhatsApp quick-reply template for same-hour first response. Introduced a daily log sheet with timestamp, customer name, issue type, and status — reviewed each shift.

Outcome

Resolution delivered within 24 hours. No repeat complaint recorded. Staff reported clearer visibility on open issues; owner could review progress without verbal check-ins.

02
Admin & logistics coordination

Preventing stock conflicts in a second-hand goods business

Barbeque  ·  Makassar, Indonesia

Context

Admin coordination sat between multi-supplier stock intake and outgoing marketplace orders. No central system tracked which items had verbal holds versus confirmed payments.

Problem

A confirmed online buyer was told an item was available — but it had already been set aside for another order. The conflict was discovered only after the buyer had been waiting.

Intervention

Added a stock status column to the existing Excel sheet (available / on-hold / sold). Every buyer enquiry was logged with a timestamp before any hold was placed. Buyers received an immediate acknowledgement while stock was confirmed.

Outcome

Stock conflicts dropped significantly within weeks. The affected buyer received priority dispatch and a personal apology. Owner gained daily stock visibility without extra team check-ins.

03
Virtual assistant — remote

Rebuilding enquiry response for a solo service operator

Private client  ·  Germany (anonymised)  ·  March 2026 – present

Context

A sole operator managing a service business handled all enquiries personally. Back-to-back appointments and time zone differences caused multi-day response delays.

Problem

A prospective client sent an enquiry, received no reply for five days, and had already booked a competitor before the client followed up. No record existed of when the message arrived.

Intervention

Took over first-response duties: all enquiries received a warm acknowledgement within 24 hours. A shared tracker was set up — accessible to both client and VA — logging each enquiry, date received, first response sent, and current status.

Outcome

Response times dropped from multiple days to under 24 hours. One prospective client specifically noted the "quick and professional response" when booking — a direct result of the new acknowledgement step.

Client feedback

"Quick and professional response" — noted by a prospective client at point of booking, attributed directly to the new first-response system.
